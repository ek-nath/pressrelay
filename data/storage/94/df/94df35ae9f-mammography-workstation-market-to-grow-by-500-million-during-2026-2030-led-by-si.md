The mammography workstation market has witnessed substantial growth recently, with projections indicating a rise from $1.13 billion in 2025 to $1.24 billion in 2026, reflecting a CAGR of 9.2%. This growth is mainly due to the increased adoption of digital mammography systems, heightened demand for early breast cancer detection, a shift from film-based imaging to digital formats, and a surge in mammography workstation deployments in medical facilities.

Looking ahead, the market is anticipated to expand further, reaching $1.74 billion by 2030, with a projected CAGR of 8.9%. This growth is expected to be driven by the adoption of advanced breast tomosynthesis workstations, integrated multimodality breast imaging, and the demand for sophisticated diagnostic tools. Government breast cancer initiatives and preferences for upgraded software and automation also contribute to this trend.

Technology advancements in AI-assisted image analysis, high-resolution displays, and multimodality diagnostic workstations are some key trends shaping the market. The rise in mammography exam volumes, largely driven by aging populations and enhanced public healthcare initiatives, further bolsters market growth. Modern imaging technologies and AI support systems simplify diagnostic processes, allowing for expedited, precise detection of anomalies.

For example, as of August 2023, the United States had 8,823 certified mammography facilities with 25,098 accredited units, according to the FDA. This illustrates the increasing demand for high-performance tools, which proficiently manage and interpret large datasets.

Major market players are focusing on innovation to refine efficiency and diagnostic accuracy. For instance, in November 2025, GE HealthCare Technologies Inc. received FDA authorization for its Pristina Recon DL technology, a deep learning-based tool enhancing 3D mammography image reconstruction. The technology stands out for its ability to improve image clarity using AI without added radiation exposure.

Corporate movements within this market include Lunit Inc.'s acquisition of Volpara Health Technologies Ltd. in May 2024. This acquisition aims to expand Lunit's cancer care solutions and leverage Volpara's extensive mammography imaging database.

Prominent companies in the sector include Siemens Healthineers AG, Fujifilm Holdings Corporation, Koninklijke Philips N.V., GE Healthcare Technologies Inc., Hologic Inc., and others. While North America was the leading region in 2025, the Asia-Pacific region is poised for rapid growth through 2030.
