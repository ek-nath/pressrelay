Malaria Diagnostics Market Revenue and Trends

The malaria diagnostics market is becoming increasingly competitive worldwide due to the continued high rate of malaria in tropical areas, increased government and non-governmental funding for malaria control programs, the need for point-of-care testing, and the availability of improved, affordable RDTs and molecular technologies in healthcare systems worldwide.

What are the Factors That Have a Significant Contribution to the Growth of the malaria diagnostics market?

The current heavy malaria burden especially in sub-Saharan Africa, where more than 90 percent of the burden is observed, and the problem of climate change, migration, and drug resistance have increased the demand for accurate and fast diagnostic products. Reports by WHO indicate that hundreds of millions of cases are still experienced every year and early diagnoses are vital in minimizing mortality. The increased global eradication efforts have led to a focus on mass testing in endemic regions, community health, and surveillance to prevent outbreaks.

Technological advancements have provided very sensitive RDTs, artificial intelligence-assisted microscopy, handheld PCR devices, and multiplex assays to enhance detection rates, comfort of use in distant locations and specificity. Other motivators are increased funding internationally by organisations such as the Global Fund, increased awareness of early diagnosis, better access to health care in rural settings and government sponsored screening and eradication efforts in the highly endemic as well as the low-transmission areas.

Segment Insight

By Product Type

As of 2025, rapid diagnostic tests (RDTs) held the largest portion of the malaria diagnostics market because they can quickly detect and identify SARS-like antigens, can be used in the field, and require little training and equipment. These tests are essential for early case detection in the community and for managing resources in a limited setting, and they are significantly influenced by technological advancements in ultra-sensitive and multiplex RDT, which health workers widely consider crucial for scaling up testing and achieving WHO elimination targets.

By Distribution Channel

The biggest market share is occupied by the public health programs and the NGO procurement channels which are the main distribution center of bulk supplies, subsidized kits, and integrated malaria control programs. As they offer training, quality assurance, and logistical support for national programs controlling high-burden endemic regions, these channels are more preferable for governments and international organizations that need to conduct the deployment of diagnostic strategies on a large scale.
